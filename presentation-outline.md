# CalmCampus Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

**CalmCampus: Student Mental Health Support App**  
Designing and evaluating a usable digital interface for university student well-being.

Speaker note: Introduce group members and explain that the project focuses on helping students access mental health support privately and easily.

## Slide 2: Problem

Many students experience stress, anxiety, loneliness, academic pressure, and fear of seeking help. Campus counseling may exist, but students may not know how to access it or may fear stigma.

Speaker note: Emphasize that the design problem is not only mental health, but access, trust, privacy, and usability.

## Slide 3: Target Users

Primary users:

- First-year students adjusting to campus.
- Final-year students under academic and career pressure.
- Student leaders who support peers.

Speaker note: Mention that the interface is mobile-first because students commonly use phones for quick support.

## Slide 4: User Research

Methods:

- Survey of students.
- Interviews with students.
- Observation of campus support access points.

Key findings:

- Academic pressure is the most common stressor.
- Privacy and trust are very important.
- Students want quick support and simple booking.
- Anonymous peer support is useful but needs safety controls.

## Slide 5: Personas

Show three personas:

- Amina, first-year student with anxiety and loneliness.
- Brian, final-year student with burnout and pressure.
- Grace, student leader who wants to refer peers to help.

Speaker note: Use one short scenario to show how each persona benefits.

## Slide 6: Ideation

Ideas considered:

- Mood tracker only.
- Counselor booking system only.
- Anonymous peer support only.
- Combined wellness app.

Chosen solution: Combined app with mood check-in, self-care, booking, chat, and SOS support.

## Slide 7: User Flow

Flow:

Open app -> Mood check-in -> Recommendation -> Self-care / booking / anonymous chat / SOS -> Confirmation -> Progress summary.

Speaker note: Explain that the flow supports users who are emotionally tired and need clear next steps.

## Slide 8: Prototype Demo

Demo steps:

1. Start mood check-in.
2. Select "Anxious".
3. Open self-care and start breathing.
4. Book counselor appointment.
5. Send anonymous chat message.
6. Open SOS support.

Speaker note: Keep the demo direct and task-based.

## Slide 9: HCI Principles Applied

- Visibility of system status.
- Recognition rather than recall.
- Consistency.
- Error prevention.
- Accessibility.
- Minimalist design.
- User control and freedom.

Speaker note: Give concrete examples from the prototype, such as confirmation messages and visible SOS.

## Slide 10: Usability Testing Phase 1

Tested with at least 5 students.

Main issues:

- SOS was not visible enough.
- Booking confirmation was unclear.
- Chat safety needed explanation.
- Mood reason tags were too many.

## Slide 11: Refinements

Changes made:

- Added fixed SOS button.
- Improved booking confirmation.
- Reduced reason tags.
- Added chat safety note and report option.
- Improved self-care recommendation.

## Slide 12: Usability Testing Phase 2

Results:

- Mood check-in success: 100%.
- Self-care success: 100%.
- Booking success: 100%.
- Chat success: 100%.
- SOS success: 100%.

Speaker note: Explain that the second test showed improved learnability and task success.

## Slide 13: Ethical and Accessibility Considerations

- App does not replace professional therapy.
- Sensitive data must be protected.
- Anonymous chat needs moderation.
- SOS must guide users to immediate real-world support.
- Interface must support readable text, high contrast, and large touch targets.

## Slide 14: Final Recommendations

For a real implementation:

- Secure student authentication or anonymous mode.
- Counselor dashboard.
- Moderated peer support.
- Emergency escalation.
- Offline self-care resources.
- Multilingual support.

## Slide 15: Conclusion

CalmCampus helps students move from stress to support through a private, simple, and accessible interface. The design improved through user research, prototyping, usability testing, and refinement.
